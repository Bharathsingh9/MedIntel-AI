# Cardiovascular Health Guidelines

## Cholesterol and Lipids
Cholesterol is a waxy substance in your blood. High levels can build up in arteries.
- Total Cholesterol: Should be under 200 mg/dL.
- LDL (Bad Cholesterol): Should be under 100 mg/dL. High LDL causes plaque buildup.
- HDL (Good Cholesterol): Should be 50 mg/dL or higher. HDL carries cholesterol back to the liver.
- Triglycerides: Should be under 150 mg/dL.

## Blood Pressure
Systolic blood pressure measures the pressure in arteries when the heart beats.
- Normal: Less than 120 mmHg
- Elevated: 120-129 mmHg
- High Blood Pressure (Hypertension Stage 1): 130-139 mmHg
- High Blood Pressure (Stage 2): 140 or higher

## Risk Reduction
1. Quit Smoking: Smoking damages blood vessels and lowers HDL cholesterol.
2. Sodium Reduction: Limit sodium to less than 2,300 mg a day.
